🛡 Fair Play: How Our Limits Protect Everyone

⏱
One Bet Per Wallet at a Time
Each wallet can only have one unresolved bet open at once. Your roll usually resolves in about two blocks (roughly 20 seconds), and then you're free to jump right back in. Human players never feel any friction, even clicking as fast as their wallet can confirm. Bots trying to flood the contract with dozens of bets at once get blocked outright.
🔒
Pool Payout Cap
No single bet can ever pay out more than 1,000,000 OTR, a fixed ceiling regardless of pool size, instead of chasing a shifting percentage. That keeps one lucky roll (or a scripted attack) from draining the pool, and it makes trying to manipulate a block pointless: whatever you'd stand to gain is smaller than the validator reward you'd be giving up.
✅
Bet Directly, No Deposits
You never send OTR to a holding account. Each bet pulls exactly your bet amount straight from your wallet the moment you roll, and winnings come right back to you. You stay in control of your OTR the whole time. The only setup is a one-time wallet approval: no registration, no account, no minimum balance.
📊
What the 2% Pool Fee Actually Does
A 2% fee only shows up in the multiplier, it shaves a small amount off your payout math, nothing else. Your roll odds are computed the exact same way every time and are never touched or adjusted based on the fee. Unlike a typical house edge, this fee stays in the pool rather than being taken out: it directly funds bankroll growth over time, and a bigger pool is what raises the max bet and max payout caps for everyone. It's a contribution to the pool, not a cut against you.
